Voltage-support limit demonstrator

← Back to line applet

This simplified applet shows why a voltage step can appear when synchronous voltage support reaches its reactive-power ceiling. Before the ceiling, voltage is held at the setpoint. After the ceiling, Q is clamped and voltage is solved from the remaining Q–V balance.

Driving variables

Voltage response

solid: operating voltage; dashed: voltage setpoint; orange: saturated; red: no high-voltage solution

Reactive-power quantities

solid: Qsync; dotted: Q required at Vref; dashed: Qmax

Line-like terms

solid: series absorption proportional to P²/V²; dashed: capacitive support proportional to V²B

Model

Before limit: V = Vref if Qrequired(Vref,P) ≤ Qmax
After limit: Qsync = Qmax and Qmax + QPE + B V² − X(P/V)² − Qload = 0
