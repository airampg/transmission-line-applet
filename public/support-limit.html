Voltage-support limit demonstrator

← Back to line applet

This simplified applet isolates the voltage step that can occur when synchronous voltage support reaches a reactive-power ceiling. The discontinuity is the change from voltage regulation to reactive-power limiting.

Driving variables

solid: operating voltage; dashed: voltage setpoint; orange: saturated; red: no high-voltage solution
solid: Qsync; dotted: Q required at Vref; dashed: Qmax
solid: series absorption; dashed: capacitive support

How to reproduce the voltage-step mechanism

1. Start from defaultsThe synchronous device regulates voltage until its Q ceiling is reached.
2. Increase selected loadingMove Selected loading to the right. The red vertical line marks the operating point.
3. Watch Q required cross QmaxIn the Reactive quantities tab, the dotted Q-required curve crosses the dashed Qmax limit.
4. Observe the voltage stepIn the Voltage step tab, the operating voltage leaves Vref and jumps to the lower post-limit solution.
5. Strengthen or weaken the gridRaise Series absorption X or lower Capacitive support B to make collapse earlier and sharper.
6. Compare fairlyUse Freeze y-axis and zoom/pan controls so changes are not hidden by dynamic axis scaling.

Interpretation: before the limit, the active constraint is V = Vref. After the limit, the active constraint is Qsync = Qmax. This removes incremental Q-V support. Under fixed P/Q behaviour, lower voltage raises current-driven X absorption and reduces V²B capacitive support.

Before limit: V = Vref if Qrequired(Vref,P) ≤ Qmax
After limit: Qsync = Qmax and Qmax + QPE + B V² − X(P/V)² − Qload = 0
